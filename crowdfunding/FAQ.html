What is MoolahSense?

MoolahSense is a web-based crowd-investing platform where people invest directly in local businesses for a fixed return, supporting the core of our economy and fostering the entrepreneurial culture of Singapore.

Using information sourced from official sources and information providers, we review all applications before businesses (corporates and limited liability partnerships) are allowed to list on the platform. Each business is identity checked and only businesses with at least 1 year of audited Financial Statements or 2 years of trading history are allowed to be listed on the platform.

Investors then select who they wish to invest in by submitting note subscription offers. Investors can spread their risks by investing across different businesses. Embodying the spirit of access, an investor can start investing from S$1000.

With MoolahSense, businesses can raise funds from many people, diversify their source of financing and become empowered to accept the lowest interest rate offers. Investors gain access to opportunities with potentially great returns and participate together with businesses in their entrepreneurship journey.

MoolahSense manages monthly repayments from the note issuer to the investors. When payments are missed, MoolahSense has a 3-stage process to recover monies from the issuer, including the use of professional third party debt collectors and proposing legal solutions to the investors. For more information please see the “Investors” or “Businesses” pages.

Why are we doing this?

We are motivated to address the gaps in access for funding small businesses and presenting steady yield opportunities to everyday investors. We hope to foster a culture of entrepreneurship and do our part to promote a more inclusive financial system.

Is MoolahSense regulated?

MoolahSense is not currently regulated by the MAS.

Are the contract notes valid?

The contract notes are enforceable in Singapore. Each contract is formed after a process of offer and acceptance occurring electronically on the platform.

Who are parties to the contract note?

The investor(s) and business issuer are contractual parties to each note. MoolahSense is not a party to the contract.

Are there any checks on the information?

Where possible, when documentary proofs are submitted, the MoolahSense team will verify the information as an independent third party. If the information disclosed by the issuer tallies with the documentary proofs that have been submitted to us, we apply a "verified" status next to the information field. We perform these checks on good faith and best effort basis. While we will do our best to verify these information, we cannot guarantee against possible frauds or other malicious intents.

What information is presented?

Each note listing shall contain the following information.

  • Business name
  • Registered and Business Address
  • The type of business e.g. private limited , limited company or limited liability partnership
  • Requested Amount
  • Target rate
  • Purpose
  • Whether a personal guarantee is provided
  • Credit Ratings provided by DP Information Group
  • Financial performance of the business including profit and loss, cash flow and financial ratios for the last 2 financial years. Exact financials are not provided, instead investors will be presented with ranges and relative ratios to provide indication of the business’ financial standing.
  • Descriptive information about the Business
  • Videos, documents and/or photos (if any)
